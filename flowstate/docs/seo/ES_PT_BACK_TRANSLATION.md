# Spanish and Brazilian Portuguese copy back-translation

Editorial QA reference for the `es-MX`, `es-ES`, and `pt-BR` marketing modules. This is not a substitute for the published Creator Music License.

## Key claims, back-translated to English

- The eligible Creator Music catalogue contains **174 tracks**: **49 City Pop**, **43 Cyberpunk Jazz**, and **82 Neo Synthwave**. **Lofi is excluded**.
- An **active Flow Pro subscription is required for each new per-track licence authorization and download**.
- Attribution is mandatory. The licence is **non-exclusive and non-transferable** and transfers neither ownership nor copyright.
- There is no stated numerical cap on **properly licensed Creator Works**. Every work must have a valid per-track authorization and comply with the required attribution and published terms.
- Creator Works first published while Pro was active remain licensed after a normal cancellation. A new work requires Pro to be active again and a new per-track authorization.
- Standalone or music-only redistribution, ownership claims or transfer, sublicensing, and registration in **Content ID** or a similar rights-management system are prohibited. Films, video games, and client work require a separate written licence.
- Listening in Flow, Spotify, or YouTube does **not** provide creator-use rights.
- Timer presets are practical planning tools, not medical advice, scientific prescriptions, or guarantees of productivity or concentration.
- Pricing copy points to the current pricing screen and checkout for the amount, billing currency, and final terms. It makes no invented claim about local currency conversion, tax, refund, or local law.

## Market distinction check

- `es-MX` uses natural Mexican-market phrasing such as *app*, *videos*, *transmisiones*, *ingresa*, *verificando* and *autorización de licencia*. `Grant Record` appears only where it is the defined product record name.
- `es-ES` uses Spain phrasing such as *aplicación*, *vídeos*, *directos*, *introduce*, *comprobando*, *divisa* and *autorización de licencia*.
- `pt-BR` is Brazilian Portuguese throughout: *você*, *app*, *timer*, *lives*, *faixas*, *baixar*, and *checkout*. It does not use European Portuguese second-person forms.
