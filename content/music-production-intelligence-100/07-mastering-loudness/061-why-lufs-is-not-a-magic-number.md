### Slide 2: SLIDE 2 | THE TRAP
Headline: The trap: [Squashing peaks] to hit numbers

Support: Squeezing transient crest factor to force a minus eight LUFS integrated level introduces transient [clipping], flattening drum envelope rise-times.

Visual: A compressed waveform with flat tops marked in warning red compared to a dynamic waveform in cyan.

### Slide 3: SLIDE 3 | THE EAR
Headline: The ear: Platforms [normalize volume] anyway

Support: [Acoustic] normalizers apply gain attenuation to high-energy masters, leaving hyper-compressed tracks perceptually quieter than dynamic arrangements.

Visual: Two faders side-by-side on a screen, one labeled loud being pulled down, the other labeled dynamic staying constant.

### Slide 4: SLIDE 4 | THE SCIENCE
Headline: How [LUFS meters] calculate energy

Support: ITU-R BS.1770-4 applies a K-weighting filter and a gate, averaging signal energy over three seconds and ignoring high-amplitude micro-[transients].

Visual: A spectral meter showing time-averaging energy blocks sliding across the screen.

### Slide 5: SLIDE 5 | THE ART
Headline: Let your [ears] set the threshold

Support: Utilize LUFS as a broad boundary metric while relying on cochlear perception to determine low-frequency headroom [saturation] limits.

Visual: A close-up of a hand turning an analog limiter threshold knob with audio meters in the background.

### Slide 6: SLIDE 6 | THE MOVE
Headline: Set limiters for [tone and punch]

Support: Calibrate the limiter threshold based on transient [saturation], ignoring the visual meter until you achieve the desired density.

Visual: A plugin interface with a threshold control slider being set by a cursor.

### Slide 7: SLIDE 7 | THE TEST
Headline: Compare versions at [matched levels]

Support: Attenuate the limited master to match the uncompressed mix. If the transient depth collapses, [back off] the threshold.

Visual: An A/B comparison panel with two gain matching sliders set at equal perceived levels.

### Slide 8: SLIDE 8 | THE CHECK
Headline: Check dynamics on [cheap speakers]

Support: Verify translation on single-driver consumer [speakers]. If inter-channel masking buries the kick, your gain reduction is excessive.

Visual: A pair of standard white earbuds next to a phone screen showing the music player.

### Slide 9: SLIDE 9 | THE RULE
Headline: [Protect the] musical movement

Support: Retain limiter settings only if the average energy increases without inducing [transient] distortion or narrowing the stereo field.

Visual: A simple diagram showing dynamic range width locked in with a cyan checkmark.

### Slide 10: SLIDE 10 | THE TAKEAWAY
Headline: [Save this] before chasing another loudness number.

Support: [Follow @virzyguns, save] this post, and visit www.virzyguns.com for daily session science.
