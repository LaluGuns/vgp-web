## Caption
Stop treating LUFS like a magic target.

When you squash a mix just to hit a specific loudness number, you destroy the transient peaks that make it punch. Plus, streaming platforms turn down loud tracks anyway. A squashed master just sounds flat and quiet next to a dynamic one.

Try this in your next session:
1. Ignore the meter until your musical balance is locked.
2. Set your limiter until the track feels solid.
3. Turn down the limited version to match the original level and compare.

If the limited version sounds smaller, back off the threshold. Trust your ears over the meter.

Save this before chasing another loudness number.
