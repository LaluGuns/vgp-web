## 10-slide script

### Slide 1: SLIDE 1 | THE HOOK
Headline: The last loudness push [steals emotion]

Support: A mix can [lose its groove] when squashed for maximum level.

Visual: A limiter threshold dial squeezing a waveform, with the compression zone highlighted.

### Slide 2: SLIDE 2 | THE TRAP
Headline: The trap: Prioritizing [level over movement]

Support: Pushing the limiter to compete with hyper-compressed masters flattens the macro-[dynamics] that define the song's emotional structure.

Visual: A flat volume curve across different song sections highlighted in warning red.

### Slide 3: SLIDE 3 | THE EAR
Headline: The ear: Human emotion needs [breathing room]

Support: Human emotional response to music relies on dynamic [contrast]. If a verse has the same loudness as a chorus, the climax fails.

Visual: A volume envelope showing high peaks and low valleys glowing in cyan.

### Slide 4: SLIDE 4 | THE SCIENCE
Headline: How limiting reduces [dynamic range]

Support: Excessive limiting reduces the [dynamic range] between sections, causing the stereo field and depth to collapse during dense parts.

Visual: A diagram showing a limiter ceiling squashing dynamic peaks down to a narrow band.

### Slide 5: SLIDE 5 | THE ART
Headline: The final push [tests your taste]

Support: Determine the limiting threshold by monitoring the emotional flow. Stop pushing gain when the song [stops breathing].

Visual: A fader control with a circular highlight around the sweet spot in cyan.

### Slide 6: SLIDE 6 | THE MOVE
Headline: Back off the threshold when [dynamics] flatline

Support: Pull back the limiter input gain at the first sign of [transient] smearing or loss of micro-dynamic movement.

Visual: A gain reduction meter showing attenuation backing off into a safe green zone.

### Slide 7: SLIDE 7 | THE TEST
Headline: Do a [blind] level matched test

Support: Compare your loud master with a version bounced three decibels quieter, level-matching both to hear which version has more [life].

Visual: An A/B test interface with gain matching faders and blind labels.

### Slide 8: SLIDE 8 | THE CHECK
Headline: Listen to [transition sections]

Support: Listen to transition sections like build-ups and drops. If the chorus does not feel wider and bigger, reduce the [limiting].

Visual: A DAW timeline highlighting the transition edit block in cyan.

### Slide 9: SLIDE 9 | THE RULE
Headline: Keep the [emotion intact]

Support: [Retain the] loudness settings only if the track gains average level without losing its emotional movement and depth.

Visual: A heart icon enclosed inside a dynamic wave shape in cyan.

### Slide 10: SLIDE 10 | THE TAKEAWAY
Headline: [Save this] before chasing another loudness number.

Support: [Follow @virzyguns, save] this post, and visit www.virzyguns.com for daily session science.

Visual: last loudness push flattening an emotion curve. Show the final decision as a clean plain-text closing frame, ready for the loop back to slide 1. Keep it premium, dark, specific, and readable in one glance.
