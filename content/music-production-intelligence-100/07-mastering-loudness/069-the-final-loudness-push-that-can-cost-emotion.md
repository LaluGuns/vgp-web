## Caption
The final loudness push can cost you the song.

Squeezing every last decibel out of your limiter might win the loudness battle, but it destroys the emotional breathing room of the arrangement. When transients are flattened, the music loses its tension and release.

Follow this process:
1. Back off the limiter threshold when the mix stops breathing.
2. Do a blind, level-matched test of the hot master against a moderate bounce.
3. Make sure the groove retains its movement.

Let the music breathe.

Save this to protect the emotion of your master.

## Pinned comment
Session tip: If the quiet bridge of your song is just as loud as the final chorus, you have over-limited. Back off the threshold and let the arrangement build.
