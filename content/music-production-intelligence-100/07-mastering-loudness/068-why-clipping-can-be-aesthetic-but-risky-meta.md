Title: Clipping can work. It can also cost you

Description: How clipping peaks provides clean headroom without limiting artifacts, and how to balance distortion risks in mastering.

Hashtags: #VGP #Mastering #LUFS #AudioEngineering #MusicProduction

CTA: Save this before chasing another loudness number.

Caption:
Peak clipping can work, but it comes with real risks.

Using a clipper to shave off transients gives you extra headroom without the pumping artifacts of a fast limiter. However, pushing it too far introduces harsh harmonic distortion that destroys the low end.

Try this in your next project:
1. Clip peak transients before they hit the limiter.
2. Listen for unwanted crackling or high end sizzle on cheap speakers.
3. Make sure the clipper acts like a precision tool, not a volume knob. Keep the clip clean.
