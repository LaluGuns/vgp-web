Title: Proximity effect can trap you

Caption:
The proximity effect is a powerful tool and a dangerous trap.

Getting too close to a directional microphone causes a massive buildup of low-frequency energy. This low-end boost, known as the proximity effect, masks the vocal's high-frequency details and makes it sound muddy.

To fix this, place a pop filter six inches from the microphone capsule to establish a physical boundary. This keeps the singer at a distance where the low-end remains natural and balanced.
