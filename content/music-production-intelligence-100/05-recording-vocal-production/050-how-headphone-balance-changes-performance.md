## Caption
What a singer hears in their headphones directly controls how they perform. If the backing track is too loud, they push and sing sharp. If they have too much reverb, they lose pitch focus and sing flat. By building a dry, vocal-focused cue mix, you trigger the brain's natural auditory feedback loop. This leads to better intonation and pocket timing automatically. Save this post for your next vocal tracking session. Follow @virzyguns and visit www.virzyguns.com for daily session science.
