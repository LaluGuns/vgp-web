Title: Consonants carry hidden emotion

Description: Aggressive de-essing and compression often destroy the emotional bite of vocal tracks. This guide outlines how transients impact speech recognition and offers a session move to balance smoothness with lyric articulation.

Caption:
While vowels carry the pitch, consonants carry the attitude.

Drowning your vocal track in aggressive de-essers strips away the high-frequency transient bursts that define speech. The brain needs these quick bursts of energy to decode words, and removing them leaves the vocal sounding slurred and lifeless.

Instead of using global de-essers, manually automate the volume of harsh sibilant clips down. This preserves the natural consonant impact while keeping the overall vocal smooth and clear.
