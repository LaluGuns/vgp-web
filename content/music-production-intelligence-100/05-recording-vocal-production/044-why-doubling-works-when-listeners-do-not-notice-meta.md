Title: Doubling works when nobody notices

Description: Loud vocal doubles clutter the mix and detract from the lead singer. This guide explains the science of auditory fusion and provides a session move to tuck double tracks so they build size without drawing attention.

Caption:
Vocal doubling works best when the listener cannot hear the second take.

When vocal doubles have obvious timing and pitch differences, the brain splits them into separate sound sources. This clutter masks the main vocal and ruins the focus of the mix instead of adding depth.

To fix this, align the double tracks tightly to the lead and lower their level. Use a low-pass filter to roll off highs above 5 kilohertz, letting the double add weight to the lead without competing for attention.
