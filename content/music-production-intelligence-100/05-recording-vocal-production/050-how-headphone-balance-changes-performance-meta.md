Title: Headphone balance changes the take

Description: Too much reverb or a loud backing track in a singer's headphones ruins pitch and timing. This guide details the science of the auditory feedback loop and offers a session move to balance cue mixes for more accurate takes.

Hashtags: #VGP #VocalProduction #RecordingVocals #MusicProduction #HomeStudio

CTA: Save this before cutting another vocal take.

Caption:
The headphone mix directly controls the singer's pitch.

High round-trip latency in the headphones disrupts the singer's internal feedback loop. Even a delay of 10 milliseconds forces the brain to adjust, causing the vocalist to sing flat and tense up their throat muscles.

To fix this, lower your DAW buffer size to 64 samples or use direct hardware monitoring. This eliminates latency, letting the singer pitch naturally and comfortably.
