Title: Perfect vocal comps can kill the human

Description: Over-editing and piecing together micro-takes can rob a vocal of its natural emotional flow. This guide explains how performance continuity affects listener believability and shows you how to comp vocals for raw feeling rather than clinical pitch.

CTA: Save this before cutting another vocal take.

Caption:
A perfect vocal comp can sound completely lifeless.

Comping syllable-by-syllable destroys the natural phrasing of a performance. The human ear easily detects the tiny, unnatural changes in vocal tract shape and breath flow that happen at artificial edit points.

To fix this, comp in full phrases or entire lines instead of words. Focus on the emotional consistency and natural dynamic flow of the take rather than trying to fix every micro-flaw.
