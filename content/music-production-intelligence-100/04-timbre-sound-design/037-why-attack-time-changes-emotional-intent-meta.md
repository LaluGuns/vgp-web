Fast compression kills transient punch.

Setting the attack time too fast on your compressor squashes the impact of the sound, making it dull. The speed of the initial transient envelope determines how the brain calculates distance and localization.

Set the compressor attack time to 30 milliseconds, letting the transient pass through before compression begins. Compare fast and slow compressor settings.
