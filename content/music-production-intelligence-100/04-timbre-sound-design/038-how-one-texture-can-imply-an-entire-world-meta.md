Adding background noise hides the song.

Stacking multiple ambient layers creates a dense wash of sound that masks the vocal and main instruments. The auditory system uses ambient textures to build a spatial reference, adjusting its sensitivity to other sounds.

Route the vocal to the sidechain input of a compressor on the ambient track, ducking the noise by 3 dB. Mute every texture except the main one to check focus.
