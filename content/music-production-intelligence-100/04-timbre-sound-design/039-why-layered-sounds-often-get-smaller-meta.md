Title: Too many layers make sounds smaller

Description: Why layered sounds often get smaller. Phase cancellation destroys low-mid power.

Hashtags: #VGP #SoundDesign #Synth #MusicProduction #AudioEngineering

CTA: Save this before stacking another layer

Caption:
Stacking duplicates creates phase mud.

Layering multiple similar sounds to build size usually introduces phase cancellations that make the track thin. Overlapping waveforms with different phase angles cancel each other out, reducing low-mid energy and punch.

Combine a low-mid body sound, a high-mid transient, and a wide ambient layer, filtering out overlapping frequencies. Sum the layered tracks to mono to check.
