Title: Louder can make the mix smaller

Description: Confusing volume with quality leads to squashed, fatigue-inducing mixes. This guide details the psychoacoustics of loudness bias and shows you how to use level-matching to make honest mixing choices.

Caption:
Louder is not always bigger.

Mixing at high volumes triggers the Fletcher-Munson effect, making you perceive more bass and treble than actually exists. This volume bias leads you to print thin, fatiguing mixes that collapse when played quietly.

To fix this, calibrate your monitors to a fixed level of 79 decibels SPL and mix at low volumes. This ensures your balance is based on true frequency relationships rather than volume-induced ear tricks.
