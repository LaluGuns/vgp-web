## Caption
A mix with reverb on everything is a mix with no depth at all.

To make a sound feel far away, you must have something else sitting right in front of the listener's face. If every element has spatial effects, your auditory field flattens into a muddy wall.

The Session Move:
1. Strip all reverb and delay from your lead vocal or main instrument.
2. Keep it completely dry and centered as your foreground anchor.
3. Apply spatial effects only to supporting elements to push them backward.

Test the relationship by toggling the lead space off. If the vocal pulls forward and separates cleanly, you have built real contrast.
