## Caption
A compressor is not just a tool to tame your loudest peaks. It is a groove shaper.

When you use compression, you are changing the transient envelope of a sound. If your attack is too fast, you kill the initial snap. If your release is too slow, the compressor never lets go, destroying the bounce.

The Session Move:
1. Turn off your makeup gain completely.
2. Adjust your attack and release times to align the compressor's pumping with the tempo.
3. Once the groove swings naturally, dial in makeup gain to match the original level.

Bypass the plugin to compare the groove. If the compressed version flows better with the beat, the settings are correct.
