## Caption
A great lead vocal does not need to be loud to feel present. It just needs its own space.

When midrange instruments fight for the exact same frequencies, cranking your vocal fader only ruins the mix balance. By ducking competing tracks only when the vocal sings, you retain vocal clarity while keeping your instrumentation full.

The Session Move:
1. Identify the primary frequency range where your lead vocal shines.
2. Route a sidechain signal from the vocal to a dynamic EQ on your instrument bus.
3. Carve a subtle dip in the instruments only when the vocal is active.

Compare the results by bypassing the dynamic EQ. If the vocal instantly pops forward without changing the volume, the pocket is set.
