Title: Expensive vocals sit in a pocket

Description: Learn how to carve a clean pocket for your lead vocal. This guide explains how to prevent instrument masking in the midrange, allowing the vocal to sit perfectly in the mix without fighting for volume. Discover the science of dynamic masking reduction.

Hashtags: #VGP #VocalProduction #MixingTips #AudioScience #HomeStudio

CTA: Save this before touching another fader.

Caption:
An expensive vocal sits in a dynamic pocket, not a static hole.

Carving a permanent, static EQ hole in your backing track to make room for the vocal makes the mix feel empty when the singer stops. It creates a disconnect between the artist and the music.

To fix this, sidechain a dynamic equalizer on the instrumental bus to the lead vocal. This cuts only the masking frequencies in the mid-range when the vocal is active, keeping the mix cohesive.
