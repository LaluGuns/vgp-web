Title: Depth needs contrast to exist

Description: Learn how to create realistic depth in your mix using contrast rather than piling on effects. This guide explains how level, tone, and ambience cues work together to place sounds in a three-dimensional field. Stop washing out your tracks with reverb and start establishing a solid foreground anchor.

Caption:
Depth in a mix is an illusion built on contrast.

Drowning every track in reverb removes the contrast between wet and dry signals. Without a dry reference point, the listener's brain cannot calculate distance, causing the entire mix to feel flat and washed out.

To fix this, keep your lead vocal dry and close, and set the reverb pre-delay above 20 milliseconds. This separates the dry transient from the wet tail, preserving the front-to-back contrast.
