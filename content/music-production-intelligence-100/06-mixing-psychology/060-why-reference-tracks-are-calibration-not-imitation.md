## Caption
A reference track is a calibration tool, not a template.

If you try to copy the exact EQ curve or loudness of a mastered commercial release, you will likely ruin the natural dynamics of your mix. The reference should reset your ears, helping you judge low-end level, vocal balance, and overall translation objectively.

The Session Move:
1. Import your reference track and pull its gain down to match your mix's volume.
2. Listen to the reference for 30 seconds to calibrate your hearing baseline.
3. Switch back to your mix and write down the first balance mismatch you hear.

Make small, targeted adjustments to your balance rather than trying to replicate the overall tone.
