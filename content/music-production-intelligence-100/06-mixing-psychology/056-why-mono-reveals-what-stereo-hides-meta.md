Caption:
Mono reveals the phase lies that stereo tries to hide.

Mixing entirely in wide stereo masks phase cancellations and frequency conflicts. When your mix plays on mono systems like phone speakers or club PAs, out-of-phase signals cancel each other out, making the mix sound thin.

To fix this, check your mix in mono early and often. Summing the master bus to mono exposes comb filtering and level imbalances, letting you resolve phase issues before adding artificial width.
