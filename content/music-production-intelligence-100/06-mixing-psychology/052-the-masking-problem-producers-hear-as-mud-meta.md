Title: Mud is often masking, not dirt

Description: Boosting your vocal fader to fight mid-range clutter just creates a harsh, messy mix. This guide explains the physics of simultaneous masking and provides a session move to carve space for the lead vocal.

Caption:
Mud in a mix is usually masking, not dirt.

When multiple instruments pile up in the 200 to 500 Hertz region, they mask each other. Boosting the highs of these tracks to find clarity only increases clutter, leaving the low-midrange sounding muddy.

To fix this, identify the secondary instruments and use a wide equalizer cut in the low-mids. This clears the masking threshold, letting the bass and vocal sit in their own clean pockets.
