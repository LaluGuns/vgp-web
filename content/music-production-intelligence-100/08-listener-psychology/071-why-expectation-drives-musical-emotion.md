## 10-slide script

### Slide 1: SLIDE 1 | THE HOOK
Headline: Expectation is the [emotion engine]

Support: The brain gets bored when a song [resolves too quickly].

Visual: A human listener reacting to a transition, with a glowing expectation curve showing tension peaks.

### Slide 2: SLIDE 2 | THE TRAP
Headline: The [trap: Satisfying] every listener guess

Support: Resolving every musical phrase immediately satisfies the listener's prediction, suppressing [dopamine] release and inducing cognitive boredom.

Visual: A straight horizontal line representing flat expectation, marked in warning red.

### Slide 3: SLIDE 3 | THE EAR
Headline: The ear: Listeners crave [delayed resolution]

Support: The human brain experiences emotional arousal when a highly anticipated auditory resolution is delayed, resolving [tension] with a reward response.

Visual: A brain wave pattern shifting from chaotic red to unified cyan.

### Slide 4: SLIDE 4 | THE SCIENCE
Headline: How [expectation drives] reward

Support: Huron's ITPRA theory demonstrates that [tension] and anticipation phases trigger physiological arousal, while delayed resolution activates neural reward centers.

Visual: A neural node diagram showing tension paths lighting up on a grid.

### Slide 5: SLIDE 5 | THE ART
Headline: Emotion [starts as prediction]

Support: Balance predictability with melodic [tension], holding back the tonic resolution to keep the listener engaged in the prediction loop.

Visual: A chord progression timeline showing the root chord delayed, highlighted in cyan.

### Slide 6: SLIDE 6 | THE MOVE
Headline: Design transitions that [delay resolution]

Support: Insert a high-pass [filter sweep] or a dynamic silence gap before the chorus, extending the anticipation phase to maximize emotional release.

Visual: A timeline view showing a drop section with a filter cutoff automation curve.

### Slide 7: SLIDE 7 | THE TEST
Headline: [Chart tension] points across the song

Support: Map the [tension] points across the arrangement. If the tension curve remains flat, alter the harmonic voicings or delay the root resolution.

Visual: A timeline grid with a dynamic intensity curve showing peaks in each chorus.

### Slide 8: SLIDE 8 | THE CHECK
Headline: [Audit pacing] with first time listeners

Support: Monitor listener pupillary response or attention shifts. If they [lose focus] before the drop, shorten the introductory pacing.

Visual: An attention spotlight icon overlaying an arrangement track view.

### Slide 9: SLIDE 9 | THE RULE
Headline: [Earn the] resolution

Support: Retain transition delays only if the climax resolves the accumulated [tension] and the release produces a satisfying emotional shift.

Visual: A checkmark next to a peak resolution icon in cyan.

### Slide 10: SLIDE 10 | THE TAKEAWAY
Headline: [Save this] before forcing emotion into the track.

Support: [Follow @virzyguns, save] this post, and visit www.virzyguns.com for daily session science.

Visual: expectation curve glowing inside a listener silhouette. Show the final decision as a clean plain-text closing frame, ready for the loop back to slide 1. Keep it premium, dark, specific, and readable in one glance.
