## Caption
Music is a prediction engine.

The brain is constantly trying to guess what note comes next. Emotional payoff happens when you delay that resolution. If you satisfy every expectation immediately, the song feels predictable and boring.

Try this in your arrangement:
1. Build structural transitions that tease the resolve before delivering it.
2. Chart the tension points across your timeline to locate flat spots.
3. Keep the listener engaged by delaying the final chorus entry.

Let the expectation build.

Save this before structuring your next transition.

## Pinned comment
Session tip: Delay your chorus by one bar of silence or a filter sweep. The brief delay builds tension and makes the final entry hit twice as hard.
