Caption:
Expensive choruses start with contrast.

Most producers stack layers and double tracks in the verse, then wonder why the chorus feels flat. The problem is auditory masking and lack of contrast. When the verse is already wide and dense, the chorus has no room to expand. The ear cannot detect a width change because there is no reference point.

The fix is perceptual contrast. Auditory scene analysis shows that the brain judges spatial width relative to what came before. Keep the verse narrow, dry, and centered. Automate the stereo width to open up on the chorus downbeat. This creates a dramatic spatial expansion that feels expensive without adding a single track.
