Title: The verse is stealing the chorus

Description: Is your verse resolving too early? Learn how to use open loops to keep listeners hooked and make your choruses feel massive.

Caption:
The verse is stealing the chorus.

When you resolve the melody and chords in the verse, you release all the tension. The listener feels satisfied and has no reason to wait for the chorus. This is the Zeigarnik effect in action: the brain craves completion, but only if the task remains open. Close the loop too early and you lose the drive.

Instead, leave the last chord hanging. End your verse on a dominant chord or a rising vocal melody. Force the listener to wait for the chorus to get the resolution they want. This creates a perceptual cliffhanger that pulls them forward into the hook.
