Caption:
One note can own the hook.

Most producers stack pitch changes hoping to find a hook. But auditory masking kicks in when the brain cannot parse each note, so the line becomes forgettable. The real science shows that rhythmic identity and timbre encode faster in memory than pitch complexity.

The move is simple: design a highly syncopated, repetitive rhythm on a single note before adding any pitch variations. Then tap the rhythm on a desk. If it still has a face without pitch, you have a hook.
