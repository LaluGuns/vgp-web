Caption:
Leave space before the title hits.

Most producers stack drums and synths right over the title line. That buries the lyric under auditory clutter. The brain cannot process competing streams at the same time. When instruments play through the title, the vocal gets masked and the hook loses impact.

The fix uses temporal expectation. Cut the drums and bass for one or two beats right before the title vocal. That sudden silence creates a vacuum. The brain locks onto the next sound because it expects resolution. The title lands harder because the ear had a clean slate.
