Caption:
Skip risk starts in the intro.

Most producers pile on synths and drums in the first three seconds. That triggers auditory masking. Simultaneous transients cancel each other out, and the ear cannot lock onto a single stream for stream segregation. The brain builds a predictive model in the first three seconds. If the pattern is rewarding, it commits. If not, it skips.

The move: Start with the strongest sonic identity. Lead with the element that has the highest spectral contrast and transient clarity. Then cut the intro second by second until the song still makes sense. Each cut removes temporal redundancy. The goal is the shortest possible intro that still triggers a clear expectation of what follows.
