Familiar chords need a new shadow

Most songwriters reach for complex jazz voicings to stand out. But the brain's working memory can only track 3-4 harmonic objects before losing the groove. Auditory stream segregation locks onto repeated patterns, so familiar chord roots anchor attention while timbre and rhythm do the fresh work.

The move is simple: change voicing, rhythm, or bass motion before changing the progression. Shift the bass to the third or fifth scale degree. This changes the harmonic context without altering the chord root, preserving listener familiarity. Strip the production to piano or guitar and test three different bass lines.
