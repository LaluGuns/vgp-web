Caption:
Lyric rhythm sells emotion first.

Most songwriters prioritize literal meaning over phonetics. They force deep lyrics into a melody without checking if the words actually sound good when sung. This creates a clunky groove that kills the emotional impact. The human voice is an instrument first. Vowel shapes carry emotional weight long before the brain decodes the text. Brain scans show that the acoustic properties of speech sounds evoke emotional responses before semantic processing begins.

The move is simple: write the cadence before polishing the words. Choose words with open, bright vowels like 'ah' or 'oh' for the highest notes in your chorus. Then test it: speak the line on one note over the beat. If a word is hard to sing or breaks the groove, change it. No matter how deep the meaning. The line starts moving the listener when the rhythm and phonetics are locked in.
