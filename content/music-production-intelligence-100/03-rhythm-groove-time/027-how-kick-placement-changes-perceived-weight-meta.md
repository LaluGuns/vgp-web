Title: Kick placement changes perceived weight

Description: How kick placement changes perceived weight. Small timing offsets between the kick and bass control the peak energy.

Hashtags: #VGP #Groove #BeatMaking #MusicProduction #Rhythm

CTA: Save this before adjusting your low end

Caption:
Grid-locked kicks can feel weak.

Snapping the kick and bass to the exact same millisecond can cause phase cancellation and transient masking. Small timing offsets between the kick transient and the bass note control the cumulative energy peak.

Move the kick track 5 milliseconds ahead of the grid to drive, or 8 milliseconds behind to add weight. Verify that the kick and bass remain distinct on speakers with limited low end extension.
