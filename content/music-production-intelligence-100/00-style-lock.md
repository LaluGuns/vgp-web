# Music production social slide system v5

This is the visual source of truth for image prompts. It allows plain footer text for the creator name, website, and tagline, but forbids invented visual symbols.

## Platform facts

- Canvas: 1080 x 1920 px, portrait 9:16.
- TikTok's official in-feed ad spec recommends vertical 9:16 and says safe zone size depends on dimensions, caption length, and added formats. Live preview can differ by device.
- TikTok says recommendation inputs include user interactions, video information such as captions, sounds, and hashtags, plus device and account settings. TikTok also says finishing a longer video from beginning to end can carry more weight than weak signals. Treat this as platform context, not a promise of reach.
- TikTok Creative Center examples favor direct before-after stories, curiosity in the first beat, close-up visual proof, and scenario framing.
- Instagram reporting on official creator guidance says views are the primary metric and sends or shares matter. Treat this as creative direction, not an algorithm guarantee.
- Instagram Reels and TikTok place UI over the video. Treat every slide as a social video frame, not a clean poster.

## Social safe layout

Use this conservative shared-safe layout for IG Reels and TikTok.

- Hard text safe zone: x 96 to 840, y 210 to 1390.
- Right UI danger zone: x 870 to 1080 from y 420 down. Keep it mostly decorative.
- Bottom caption danger zone: y 1540 to 1920. Do not put important text there.
- Top app danger zone: y 0 to 170. Keep only background texture there.
- Footer text zone: render only the listed footer text as plain, centered typography around y 1435 to 1515. Allowed footer text: `www.virzyguns.com` and `100% Art. 100% Science.` Do not place any symbol, stylized V, triangular V, initials, icon, mark, emblem, badge, crest, or decorative shape above, between, beside, or near the footer text.
- Lower-left photo overlay area: x 80 to 360, y 1180 to 1510. Keep this open unless the prompt asks for a producer or human moment.
- Main visual can bleed into danger zones, but headline, support line, labels, references, and footer cannot.

## Non-negotiables

- Create one single standalone Instagram or TikTok slide.
- Do not create a grid, collage, multi-panel preview, YouTube thumbnail, or watermark.
- Do not use anime, cartoon, grunge, fake plugin brands, fake DAW brands, fake exact numbers, fake scientific charts, or clutter.
- Do not use purple or green thumbnail colors.
- All visible text must match the slide script exactly.
- Do not add extra words, extra labels, random numbers, fake meters, fake UI copy, stylized V, triangular V, initials logo, monogram, icon, emblem, badge, mark, crest, symbol, watermark shape, or decorative brand shape. Plain text `www.virzyguns.com` and `100% Art. 100% Science.` is allowed only when listed in Visible text.
- Every slide needs one motion cue that can be animated in about 3 seconds. For Slide 1 (THE HOOK), the motion cue must complete the pattern interrupt in the first 0.5 seconds.

## Typography system

- Headline sits in the upper safe zone and gets the most space. First slide headline must be readable at thumbnail size (200px wide preview).
- Headline size target: 100 to 140 px equivalent on 1080 x 1920.
- Support line size target: 38 to 48 px equivalent.
- Pill label size target: 24 to 30 px equivalent.
- Reference line size target: 18 to 22 px equivalent.
- Use Apple SF Pro Display for headlines and SF Pro Text for support lines. If SF Pro is unavailable, use Helvetica Neue or Inter. Do not use condensed, serif, gamer, handwritten, or decorative fonts.
- Use white for normal headline text.
- Accent the headline and the support line separately. The CTA slide also gets one CTA accent.
- Each accent phrase should be 1 to 4 words when possible. Never accent a full headline or full support sentence.
- Use cyan or electric-blue gradient for headline accents, ice-blue underline for support accents, and a small cyan underline for CTA accents. Warm amber or soft red-orange accent permitted on Slide 1 (THE HOOK) only for maximum pattern interrupt.
- Keep body text out of pills. Pills are for labels and badges only.
- Wrap long headlines into 2 to 4 balanced lines. Do not shrink text until it looks cheap.

## Core look

- Style: premium dark futuristic Apple editorial.
- Visual world: music production science lab, not gamer cyberpunk.
- Background: deep black-blue with a subtle dotted grid and faint waveform texture.
- Palette: black-blue, cyan, electric blue, ice blue, white, muted grey.
- Red is reserved for masking, clipping, warning, crowded zones, or bad decisions.
- Materials: glass panels, thin cyan strokes, soft bloom, restrained reflections.
- Use one hero object per slide. Avoid dashboards full of tiny unreadable widgets.
- Keep at least 30 percent negative space. Slide 1 (THE HOOK) may use up to 40% less negative space for higher visual density and scroll-stop impact.
- Do not put cards inside cards.

## Composition formula

- Top-left safe zone: small slide label.
- Top-right safe zone: small category text only if needed. Do not use an icon or badge.
- Upper-middle safe zone: headline with one accented phrase.
- Middle safe zone: hero visual, diagram, DAW lane, waveform, ear or brain path, or A/B object.
- Lower-middle safe zone: one compact insight or support line.
- Footer text zone: render only the listed footer text as plain, centered typography around y 1435 to 1515. Allowed footer text: `www.virzyguns.com` and `100% Art. 100% Science.` Do not place any symbol, stylized V, triangular V, initials, icon, mark, emblem, badge, crest, or decorative shape above, between, beside, or near the footer text.
- Danger zones: use only background glow, decorative waveform, or cropped visual energy.

## Retention structure

- Slide 1: hard pattern interrupt, instant problem, strong thumbnail read.
- Slides 2 and 3: symptom and before-after contrast.
- Slides 4 to 6: science mechanism, human taste decision, practical move.
- Slides 7 and 8: test and translation check.
- Slides 9 and 10: rule and loopable brand close.

## Slide archetypes

- THE HOOK: big pain, one dramatic visual, strong accented phrase.
- THE TRAP: bad decision in red and cyan, visual pressure, no tiny text.
- THE EAR: one listener path, one lead signal, support layers around it.
- THE SCIENCE: one clean concept diagram, no fake numbers.
- THE ART: human taste decision, desk, knob, lane choice, or emotional judgment.
- THE MOVE: one production action shown clearly.
- THE TEST: A/B switch, mute check, level-match check, or playback comparison.
- THE CHECK: phone, car, earbuds, studio, mono, export, or reset check.
- THE RULE: one clean rule, lots of negative space.
- THE TAKEAWAY: saveable final frame with allowed plain footer text, but no symbol logo, monogram, emblem, badge, or invented mark.

## Pillar visual language

- Songwriting intelligence: lyric notebook, phone voice memo, title spotlight, melody contour, hook text, silence before the title.
- Arrangement and energy: DAW lanes, mute states, energy curves, density ladders, section jumps, chorus weight.
- Rhythm, groove, and time: timing grid, microtiming dots, swing arc, body pulse, drum pad pressure, head-nod motion.
- Timbre and sound design: synth macro, spectral fingerprint, envelope sculpture, harmonic glow, texture particles, small speaker clues.
- Recording and vocal production: microphone, comp lane, consonant spike, breath marker, headphone cue, singer distance.
- Mixing psychology: DAW screenshot feel, masking heat, plugin-like panels, fader moves, mono fold, depth stage, A/B test.
- Mastering and loudness: limiter, LUFS meter, true peak ceiling, codec export, gain matching, playback translation.
- Listener psychology: human listener scene, memory trace, expectation curve, attention spotlight, emotional color shift.
- Producer psychology: desk state, timer, decision board, reference mirror, plugin overload, reset cue, momentum checkpoint.
- DSP, math, and physics: waveform-to-spectrum, sampling grid, phase wheel, filter slope, impulse response, noise floor.

## Text beauty rules

- The slide should read in under 3 seconds.
- Never let text touch the right UI danger zone.
- Never place the support line in the bottom caption danger zone.
- Avoid centered paragraphs. Use confident left alignment for headline and support, but center the footer text horizontally in the footer text zone.
- The headline accent should create the thumbnail read even if the viewer sees the slide for one second.
- The support accent should mark the proof, test, or constraint, not repeat the headline.
- Keep the reference line tiny and muted. It is there for credibility, not decoration.

## No-symbol rule

- Keep plain text `www.virzyguns.com` and `100% Art. 100% Science.` when listed in Visible text.
- Do not interpret the creator name, website, tagline, or the letter V as a logo request.
- Never draw a stylized V, triangular V, initials logo, monogram, icon, emblem, badge, mark, crest, symbol, watermark shape, or decorative brand shape.
- Visible text is limited to the exact lines inside each prompt.
- Ignore hashtags, captions, descriptions, source names, and metadata while generating the image.
