## Caption
Before you reach for an EQ to fix a weak, hollow sound, check your phase.

If you have two microphones on a guitar cabinet, a snare, or a kick drum, they record the soundwaves at slightly different times. If the crest of one wave meets the trough of another, they cancel each other out. Stacking EQ boosts on a canceled frequency is like throwing fuel into a black hole.

The play: Flip the polarity of one track. If the low-end returns, keep it flipped. Then, nudge the timing by a few milliseconds until the signals lock together.

Align the timing first, EQ second. Save this to keep your low-end solid tonight. Follow @virzyguns and visit www.virzyguns.com for daily session science.
