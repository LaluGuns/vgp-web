### Slide 3: SLIDE 3 | THE EAR
Headline: [Comb filtering] hollows the sound
Support: When two microphones capture the same source at different distances, the signals arrive at different times. Summing them causes comb filtering, which hollows out the frequency [response].

Headline: [Wave interference]
Support: Combining waveforms with a phase angle offset leads to constructive and destructive interference. At 180 degrees offset, identical [frequencies] cancel each other out completely.

### Slide 5: SLIDE 5 | THE ART
Headline: [Phase alignment] creates depth
Support: Aligning microphones creates a solid center image. Use [phase alignment] to build body, and use tiny phase offsets to create width.

Headline: Nudge [waveforms] to align phase
Support: Zoom in on multi-mic tracks like snare top and bottom. Nudge the waveforms until the [transient] peaks align, or flip the polarity switch to test the low-end response.

Headline: The [polarity flip] check
Support: Flip the polarity of one track. If the low [frequencies] disappear, flip it back. If the sound gets fuller, keep the polarity inverted.
