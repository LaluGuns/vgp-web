Caption:
Harsh digital high-end is often just aliasing.

When non-linear processors like saturators or clippers generate harmonics exceeding half the sample rate, these frequencies cannot be represented digitally. Instead, they fold back into the audible spectrum as harsh, unharmonic noise.

The play: Enable oversampling on non-linear plugins. Raising the internal sample rate shifts the folding point high above the audible range, allowing digital filters to discard the excess harmonics.
