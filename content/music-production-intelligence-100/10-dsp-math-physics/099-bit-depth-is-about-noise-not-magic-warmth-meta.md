Bit depth is noise headroom.

Bit depth does not add warm harmonic saturation or frequency detail. Instead, it determines the dynamic range ceiling of your digital signal, with each bit adding 6 dB of headroom above the quantization noise floor.

The play: Export your session at 24-bit. Apply triangular dither when reducing bit depth to convert harsh quantization distortion into a harmless, low-level randomized hiss.
