Caption:
Filters are shape machines.

High-passing every track to clean up mud causes phase smearing. Digital minimum-phase equalisers sum delayed copies of a signal to cut frequencies, introducing time delay and softening transients.

The play: Use gentle 6 dB or 12 dB per octave filter slopes. Better yet, apply a low-shelf filter to reduce low-frequency energy without messing with transient phase timing.
