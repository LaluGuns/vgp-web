Caption:
Professional songs ride an energy curve.

Most producers keep the drums and synths at the same intensity from verse to chorus. This flat energy line bores the listener because the auditory system habituates to constant stimulation. The brain tracks emotional engagement through dynamic changes in energy and complexity over time.

The move is to draw the energy curve before adding sounds. Use a density ladder to plan the exact number of active instruments in each section. Rate each section from one to five before mixing. Verify that the verse and chorus are not at the same level. Check every transition point for a clear change in texture, width, or frequency content.
