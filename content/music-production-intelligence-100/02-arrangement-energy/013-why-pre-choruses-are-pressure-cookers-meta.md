Caption:
Pre-chorus is pressure, not filler.

Your chorus feels small because your pre-chorus is too big. If you don't build tension first, the release won't hit. The ear uses auditory stream segregation to track tension and release. When you let the pre-chorus get as wide and heavy as the chorus, you kill the surprise and flatten the drop. The brain expects a payoff after tension. Without it, the chorus feels weak.

Use the pre-chorus as a pressure cooker: cut the low end with a high-pass filter, narrow the stereo field, and tighten the rhythm. This creates spectral and temporal contrast that makes the chorus feel massive by comparison. Mute the kick and bass for the last four bars of the pre-chorus to create a physical drop in energy. The chorus opens harder because the ear finally gets the release it was waiting for.
