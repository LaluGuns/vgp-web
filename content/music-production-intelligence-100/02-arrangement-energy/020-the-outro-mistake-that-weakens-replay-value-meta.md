Caption:
Outro clutter kills the replay.

The recency effect dictates that listeners judge a song by its ending. If your outro drags, the replay value dies. A 30-second fade over a repetitive loop triggers auditory habituation. The brain stops attending and the song feels flat. Auditory stream segregation means the last clear event defines the song's perceived structure. A weak tail rewrites the memory.

End the track on a clean hit while the energy is still high. Place the final cut on the last chorus downbeat or a clean vocal tail. The transient resets the listener's temporal integration window. Remove the final 8-bar repeat. The sudden silence creates a contrast that makes the hook feel more urgent. Check the final decay tail. A reverb tail longer than 2 seconds creates spectral masking that buries the final transient. Trim it to keep the ending punchy.
