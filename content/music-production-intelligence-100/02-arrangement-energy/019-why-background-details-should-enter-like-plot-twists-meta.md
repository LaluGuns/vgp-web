Caption:
Background details need plot twist timing.

Ear candy is useless if it runs all the time. It just becomes background noise. The trick is timing: treat details like plot twists. Introduce them only in the gaps where the vocal leaves space. This keeps the track developing without crowding the mix.

The brain is designed to ignore continuous background noise but immediately processes new, salient details that enter the field. This is driven by the reticular activating system and the mismatch negativity response in the auditory cortex. A 200-300ms gap is enough to reset the perceptual grouping of the background layer.

Session move: Automate your ear candy to play only in the gaps between vocal phrases. Use volume automation to bring the detail up during the final two beats of each vocal line, then pull it back when the vocal re-enters.
