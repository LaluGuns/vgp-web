Caption:
Intros die when they explain too much.

Most producers play the full hook and beat in the first ten seconds. This floods the listener's auditory stream with too many elements, causing early habituation and skip behavior. The brain's attention budget is spent before the vocal even arrives.

The fix is simple: tease the identity without playing the full answer. Use a low-pass filter, chop the melody, or play a simplified version. This creates a curiosity gap that keeps the listener locked in until the chorus payoff.
