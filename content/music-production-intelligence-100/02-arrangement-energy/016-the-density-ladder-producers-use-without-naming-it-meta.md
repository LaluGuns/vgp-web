Caption:
Density ladder before another layer.

If your song feels like one long loop, you need a density ladder. Don't let your verse and chorus have the same weight. The brain groups sounds into streams through auditory grouping. When too many streams compete at once, the arrangement falls apart. Plan your sections so the density climbs step-by-step. Keep the verse sparse so the chorus has room to hit.

The move: Assign each section a density step. Set a strict track limit for each section (e.g. 3 leads in verse, 5 in pre-chorus, 7 in chorus). Count the active melodic and rhythmic elements in each section to ensure a clear step-up structure. Verify that the transition between steps feels like a clear jump in density rather than a gradual slide.
