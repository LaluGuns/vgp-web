Caption:
Remove one layer. Make the drop heavier

Stacking more layers on your drop is actually making it sound smaller. More tracks mean more masking and less headroom. By muting the layers that clash with your lead, you give your kick, snare, and bass room to breathe.

The science is auditory masking. When two sounds occupy the same frequency range at the same time, the brain cannot separate them. This makes the mix feel smaller and the drop lose its punch. The fix is not adding more layers. It is removing the ones that copy the lead job.

The session move is simple. Mute each support layer one by one during the busiest section of your drop. Find the layers that are adding mud. Remove them. The drop gets a clear front edge.
