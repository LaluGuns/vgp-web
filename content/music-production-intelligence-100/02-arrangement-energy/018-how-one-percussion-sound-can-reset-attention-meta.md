Caption:
One tiny percussion hit can reset attention

Stop using massive snare rolls to transition between sections. They are predictable and clutter the mix. The brain filters out repetitive loops through a process called auditory stream segregation, but a single unexpected transient triggers an orienting response that resets focus.

Instead, use a single unique percussion sound. A tiny woodblock or rimshot on the last beat resets the ear without breaking the groove. Place it exactly where the arrangement leaves a pocket of space. This leverages the novelty and timing mechanisms that keep listeners engaged.
