Caption:
Second verse needs mutation, not clutter.

Adding more synths and pads to the second verse triggers spectral masking and comb filtering. This buries the vocal and kills headroom for the chorus. The brain groups identical rhythmic patterns as one object via auditory stream segregation, then habituates and stops processing them.

Instead, change the rhythm of existing bass or drums. This alters the transient timing, which resets the listener's temporal integration window and re-engages attention. Mutate the bassline rhythm or swap the hi-hat pattern for a shaker in the second verse to shift the attack transient spectrum and avoid auditory masking.
